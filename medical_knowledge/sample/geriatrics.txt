# Geriatric Medicine Guidelines

## Falls Assessment
### History
- "SPLATT": Symptoms, Previous falls, Location, Activity, Time, Trauma.
- Mechanical (trip) vs. Medical (syncope/dizziness).
### Risk Factors
- Intrinsic: Visual impairment, muscle weakness, gait disorder, hypotension.
- Extrinsic: Loose rugs, poor lighting, ill-fitting footwear.
### Management
- Check lying and standing blood pressure (Postural Hypotension).
- Review medications (sedatives, antihypertensives).
- Physiotherapy referral for strength and balance training.
- Bone health: Consider Osteoporosis risk (FRAX score).

## Dementia vs. Delirium
### Delirium (Acute Confusional State)
- **Onset**: Acute (hours/days).
- **Course**: Fluctuating.
- **Consciousness**: Altered/clouded.
- **Causes ("PINCH ME")**: Pain, Infection, Nutrition, Constipation, Hydration, Medication, Environment.
- **Management**: Treat underlying cause.

### Dementia (Major Neurocognitive Disorder)
- **Onset**: Insidious (months/years).
- **Course**: Progressive decline.
- **Consciousness**: Generally clear until late stages.
- **Types**: Alzheimer's (memory loss), Vascular (step-wise decline), Lewy Body (hallucinations, Parkinsonism).

## Polypharmacy
### Definition
- Concurrent use of multiple medications (usually 5+).
### Risks
- Adverse Drug Reactions (ADRs).
- Falls (especially with benzodiazepines, opiates, anticholinergics).
- Reduced medication adherence.
### Deprescribing Strategy
- Identify medications with no clear indication.
- Review "ACB Score" (Anticholinergic Burden).
- Stop medications where harm outweighs benefit (e.g., statins in end-of-life care).
