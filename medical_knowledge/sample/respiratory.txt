# Respiratory Assessment Guidelines

## Common Presenting Complaints
- Cough (productive or dry)
- Shortness of breath (dyspnoea)
- Wheeze
- Chest tightness
- Haemoptysis

## COPD Exacerbation
### Symptoms
- Increased breathlessness
- Increased sputum volume
- Increased sputum purulence

### Management
- Bronchodilators: Salbutamol 5mg nebulised
- Steroids: Prednisolone 30mg for 5 days
- Antibiotics if purulent sputum: Amoxicillin 500mg TDS or Doxycycline 200mg stat then 100mg OD

## Asthma
### Assessment
- Peak flow measurement
- Oxygen saturation
- Ability to complete sentences
- Respiratory rate
- Heart rate

### Severity Classification
- Mild: PEF >75% predicted
- Moderate: PEF 50-75% predicted
- Severe: PEF 33-50% predicted
- Life-threatening: PEF <33% predicted

### Treatment
- Salbutamol 100mcg MDI 4-10 puffs via spacer
- Consider oral prednisolone 40-50mg
- Oxygen to maintain SpO2 94-98%

## Safety Netting
Advise patient to return if:
- Symptoms worsen despite treatment
- Unable to complete sentences
- Using reliever inhaler more frequently
- Peak flow drops significantly
