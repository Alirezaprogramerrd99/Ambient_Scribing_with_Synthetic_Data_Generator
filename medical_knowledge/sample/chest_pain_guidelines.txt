# Chest Pain Clinical Guidelines

## Overview
Chest pain is a common presentation in primary care requiring systematic assessment to identify serious causes while appropriately managing benign conditions.

## Red Flags (Require Immediate Action)
- Severe crushing central chest pain
- Pain radiating to jaw, left arm, or back
- Associated shortness of breath
- Sweating (diaphoresis)
- Nausea or vomiting with chest pain
- Syncope or near-syncope
- Hypotension (systolic BP <90mmHg)
- New heart murmur
- Unequal blood pressure in both arms (aortic dissection)
- Pain worse on inspiration with tachycardia (pulmonary embolism)

## Differential Diagnosis

### Cardiac Causes
- Acute coronary syndrome (ACS) - STEMI, NSTEMI, unstable angina
- Stable angina pectoris
- Pericarditis
- Myocarditis
- Aortic dissection

### Respiratory Causes
- Pulmonary embolism
- Pneumonia
- Pneumothorax
- Pleurisy

### Gastrointestinal Causes
- Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GORD/GERD)
- Oesophageal spasm
- Peptic ulcer disease

### Musculoskeletal Causes
- Costochondritis
- Muscle strain
- Rib fracture

## Investigations

### First-Line Investigations
- 12-lead ECG (within 10 minutes if ACS suspected)
- Troponin I or Troponin T (high-sensitivity)
- Full blood count (FBC/CBC)
- Urea and electrolytes (U&E)
- Blood glucose
- Chest X-ray

### Second-Line Investigations
- D-dimer (if PE suspected)
- BNP or NT-proBNP (heart failure)
- CT pulmonary angiogram (CTPA) - for PE
- CT aorta - for dissection
- Echocardiogram
- Exercise stress test
- Coronary angiography

## Management

### Acute Coronary Syndrome
- Aspirin 300mg stat (chew)
- GTN sublingual spray
- Morphine 2.5-5mg IV for pain
- Oxygen only if SpO2 <94%
- Dual antiplatelet therapy: Aspirin + Clopidogrel/Ticagrelor
- Anticoagulation: Fondaparinux or Enoxaparin
- Beta-blocker: Bisoprolol or Metoprolol
- ACE inhibitor: Ramipril or Lisinopril
- Statin: Atorvastatin 80mg

### Stable Angina
- GTN sublingual for acute relief
- Beta-blocker: Bisoprolol 2.5-10mg daily
- Or Calcium channel blocker: Amlodipine 5-10mg daily
- Aspirin 75mg daily
- Statin: Atorvastatin 20-80mg daily
- Consider referral for angiography

### Musculoskeletal Chest Pain
- Paracetamol 1g QDS
- Ibuprofen 400mg TDS (if no contraindications)
- Reassurance and explanation
- Local heat application
- Physiotherapy if persistent

### GORD-Related Chest Pain
- Proton pump inhibitor: Omeprazole 20mg daily or Lansoprazole 30mg daily
- Lifestyle advice: weight loss, avoid late meals, elevate head of bed
- Avoid trigger foods: caffeine, alcohol, spicy foods

## Safety Netting Advice
Tell patients to seek immediate medical attention if they experience:
- Chest pain lasting more than 15 minutes
- Pain spreading to arm, jaw, neck, or back
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Feeling sick, sweaty, or lightheaded
- Sudden severe pain
- Chest pain with collapse or blackout

## Follow-up
- Review within 2 weeks for new chest pain
- Cardiac rehabilitation referral post-ACS
- Annual cardiovascular risk assessment
- Medication review at 4-6 weeks
