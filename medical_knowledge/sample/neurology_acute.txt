# Acute Neurology Guidelines

## Stroke and TIA
### FAST Assessment
- **F**ace: Facial weakness (can they smile?).
- **A**rms: Arm weakness (can they raise both arms?).
- **S**peech: Slurred speech (can they repeat a sentence?).
- **T**ime: Time is brain. Call emergency services immediately.

### Transient Ischaemic Attack (TIA)
- Symptoms resolve completely within 24 hours (usually <1 hour).
- **Risk**: High risk of full stroke in following days.
- **Management**: Aspirin 300mg immediately (after excluding hemorrhage if possible), urgent referral to TIA clinic, ABCD2 score assessment.

### Acute Stroke Management
- Maintain oxygen saturation >95%.
- Check blood glucose (rule out hypoglycaemia mimicking stroke).
- Urgent CT Head to differentiate Ischaemic vs Haemorrhagic.
- Thrombolysis (Alteplase) consideration if <4.5 hours from onset.

## Seizures and Epilepsy
### Acute Seizure Management
1. **Safety**: Protect head, do not restrain, do not put anything in mouth.
2. **Timing**: Time the seizure duration.
3. **Status Epilepticus**: Seizure >5 minutes or recurrent seizures without recovery.
   - **First line**: Buccal Midazolam (10mg) or Rectal Diazepam.
   - **Second line**: IV Lorazepam (if access available).

### Post-Ictal State
- Confusion, drowsiness, or headache following a seizure.
- Monitor vitals and GCS until full recovery.

### Red Flags
- First-ever seizure.
- Seizure lasting >5 minutes.
- History of recent head trauma.
- Persistent neurological deficit (Todd's Paresis).
