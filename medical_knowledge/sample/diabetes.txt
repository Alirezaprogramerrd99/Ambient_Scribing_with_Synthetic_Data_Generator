# Diabetes Management Guidelines

## Overview
Diabetes mellitus is a chronic metabolic condition characterised by hyperglycaemia.
Type 2 diabetes accounts for 90% of cases and is increasingly common.

## Diagnostic Criteria
- HbA1c ≥48 mmol/mol (6.5%)
- Fasting glucose ≥7.0 mmol/L
- Random glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L with symptoms
- OGTT 2-hour glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L

## Red Flags - Urgent Action Required
- DKA: glucose >14, ketones >3, pH <7.3, drowsiness, vomiting
- HHS: glucose >30, severe dehydration, confusion
- Hypoglycaemia: glucose <4, confusion, seizures, unconsciousness
- New foot ulcer or infection

## Target Levels

### HbA1c Targets
- Type 2 on lifestyle/metformin alone: 48 mmol/mol (6.5%)
- Type 2 on agents causing hypoglycaemia: 53 mmol/mol (7.0%)
- Individualise for frail/elderly: up to 64 mmol/mol (8.0%)

### Blood Glucose
- Fasting: 4-7 mmol/L
- Pre-meal: 4-7 mmol/L
- 2 hours post-meal: <8.5 mmol/L

### Blood Pressure
- Target: <140/80 mmHg
- With renal disease: <130/80 mmHg

### Lipids
- Total cholesterol: <4 mmol/L
- LDL cholesterol: <2 mmol/L

## Type 2 Diabetes Management

### Step 1: Lifestyle + Metformin
- Diet: reduce refined carbohydrates, increase fibre
- Exercise: 150 minutes moderate activity per week
- Weight loss: target 5-10% body weight
- Metformin: start 500mg OD, titrate to 1g BD

### Step 2: Dual Therapy (if HbA1c remains ≥58)
Add one of:
- SGLT2 inhibitor (e.g., Empagliflozin 10mg) - especially if CVD/HF
- GLP-1 agonist (e.g., Semaglutide) - especially if BMI ≥35
- DPP-4 inhibitor (e.g., Sitagliptin 100mg)
- Sulphonylurea (e.g., Gliclazide 40-160mg)
- Pioglitazone 15-30mg

### Step 3: Triple Therapy or Insulin
- Consider insulin if HbA1c remains ≥58 despite dual therapy
- Start with basal insulin (e.g., Lantus, Levemir)
- Initial dose: 10 units or 0.1-0.2 units/kg

## Annual Review Checklist
- HbA1c
- Blood pressure
- Lipid profile
- Renal function (eGFR, ACR)
- Foot examination
- Eye screening (retinopathy)
- BMI/weight
- Smoking status
- Injection site examination (if applicable)
- Hypoglycaemia review

## Sick Day Rules
- Never stop metformin if eating/drinking
- STOP SGLT2 inhibitors during acute illness
- Monitor glucose more frequently
- Stay hydrated
- Seek help if vomiting, ketones, or unable to eat

## Safety Netting
Advise patient to seek urgent help if:
- Persistent vomiting or unable to keep fluids down
- Blood glucose >20 mmol/L with symptoms
- Blood glucose <4 mmol/L not responding to treatment
- Signs of infection (fever, new foot wound)
- Confusion or drowsiness
- Chest pain or breathlessness
