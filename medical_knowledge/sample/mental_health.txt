# Mental Health Assessment Guidelines

## Overview
Mental health conditions are common, affecting 1 in 4 people. Primary care plays 
a crucial role in identification, initial management, and ongoing support.

## Red Flags - Urgent Action Required
- Active suicidal ideation with plan
- Psychotic symptoms (hallucinations, delusions)
- Severe self-neglect
- Risk to others
- Severe depression with inability to function
- Manic episode

## Depression Assessment

### Core Symptoms (at least one required)
- Persistent low mood
- Loss of interest/pleasure (anhedonia)
- Fatigue/low energy

### Additional Symptoms
- Poor concentration
- Low self-esteem
- Guilt/worthlessness
- Sleep disturbance
- Appetite/weight change
- Psychomotor changes
- Suicidal thoughts

### Severity Classification
- Mild: 5+ symptoms, minor functional impairment
- Moderate: 5+ symptoms, moderate functional impairment
- Severe: Most symptoms, marked functional impairment, may have psychotic features

### Screening Tools
- PHQ-9: Score ≥10 suggests moderate depression
- GAD-7: For concurrent anxiety assessment

## Anxiety Disorders

### Generalised Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
- Excessive worry about multiple areas for ≥6 months
- Difficulty controlling worry
- Associated: restlessness, fatigue, concentration problems, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance

### Panic Disorder
- Recurrent unexpected panic attacks
- Persistent concern about future attacks
- Avoidance behaviours

### Social Anxiety
- Marked fear of social situations
- Fear of negative evaluation
- Avoidance of social situations

## Management

### Mild-Moderate Depression
- Self-help resources, bibliotherapy
- Computerised CBT (e.g., Beating the Blues)
- Exercise programmes
- Consider antidepressant if persistent

### Moderate-Severe Depression
- Antidepressant + psychological therapy
- First-line: SSRI (e.g., Sertraline 50mg, Citalopram 20mg)
- Continue for 6+ months after remission

### Anxiety Disorders
- First-line: Self-help, psychoeducation
- CBT (individual or group)
- SSRI if moderate-severe (e.g., Sertraline 50mg)
- Pregabalin for GAD if SSRI not tolerated

## Antidepressant Prescribing

### SSRIs (First-line)
- Sertraline 50mg (safest in cardiac disease)
- Citalopram 20mg (max 40mg, 20mg if >65)
- Fluoxetine 20mg (long half-life, activating)

### Other Options
- Mirtazapine 15-45mg (sedating, weight gain)
- Venlafaxine 75-225mg (monitor BP)
- Duloxetine 60mg (also for neuropathic pain)

### Important Counselling Points
- Takes 2-4 weeks to feel benefit
- Continue for 6+ months minimum
- Do not stop abruptly (taper)
- Common side effects: nausea, headache, initial anxiety increase
- Warn about increased anxiety/suicidal ideation in early weeks (young people)

## Suicide Risk Assessment
- Ask directly about suicidal thoughts
- Assess frequency, intensity, plans, access to means
- Protective factors: social support, reasons for living
- Previous attempts (strongest risk factor)
- Document risk assessment clearly

## Safety Netting
Advise patient/family to seek urgent help if:
- Suicidal thoughts or plans
- Self-harm
- Unable to care for self
- Symptoms significantly worsen
- Unusual thoughts or behaviours
- Side effects causing concern

## Crisis Resources
- NHS 111
- Samaritans: 116 123
- Crisis team (local)
- A&E if immediate risk
