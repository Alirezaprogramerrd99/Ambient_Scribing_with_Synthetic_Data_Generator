# Hypertension Management Guidelines

## Overview
Hypertension affects approximately 1 in 4 adults and is a major risk factor for 
cardiovascular disease, stroke, and chronic kidney disease.

## Diagnosis
- Clinic BP ≥140/90 mmHg on 2 separate occasions
- Confirm with ABPM (average ≥135/85) or HBPM (average ≥135/85)
- Stage 1: Clinic 140-159/90-99, ABPM/HBPM 135-149/85-94
- Stage 2: Clinic 160-179/100-109, ABPM/HBPM 150-169/95-104
- Stage 3 (Severe): Clinic ≥180/110

## Red Flags - Urgent Referral
- Accelerated hypertension (BP ≥180/120 with signs of papilloedema/retinal haemorrhage)
- Suspected phaeochromocytoma (episodic headache, sweating, palpitations)
- New onset in young patient (<40 years)
- Resistant hypertension (uncontrolled on 3+ agents)
- Suspected secondary cause

## Target Blood Pressure
- Age <80: Clinic <140/90, ABPM/HBPM <135/85
- Age ≥80: Clinic <150/90, ABPM/HBPM <145/85
- With diabetes: <140/80 (consider <130/80 if tolerated)
- With CKD: <140/90 (or <130/80 if ACR ≥70)

## Initial Assessment
- Confirm diagnosis with ABPM/HBPM
- Assess cardiovascular risk (QRISK3)
- Check for end-organ damage
- Exclude secondary causes
- Bloods: U&E, HbA1c, lipids, eGFR
- Urine: ACR
- ECG: LVH assessment

## Treatment Algorithm

### Step 1
- Age <55 or T2DM: ACE inhibitor (e.g., Ramipril 2.5-10mg) or ARB
- Age ≥55 or Black African/Caribbean: CCB (e.g., Amlodipine 5-10mg)

### Step 2 (if BP not at target)
- ACEi/ARB + CCB

### Step 3 (if BP still not at target)
- ACEi/ARB + CCB + Thiazide-like diuretic (e.g., Indapamide 2.5mg)

### Step 4 (Resistant hypertension)
- Consider low-dose spironolactone 25mg (if K+ ≤4.5)
- Or alpha-blocker (e.g., Doxazosin 4-8mg)
- Or beta-blocker (e.g., Bisoprolol 5-10mg)

## Lifestyle Modifications
- Reduce salt intake (<6g/day)
- DASH diet: fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy
- Weight loss if overweight (target BMI <25)
- Exercise: 150 minutes moderate activity per week
- Limit alcohol: ≤14 units/week
- Smoking cessation
- Reduce caffeine intake

## Monitoring
- Review within 4 weeks of starting/changing treatment
- Once stable, review every 6-12 months
- Check U&E 1-2 weeks after starting ACEi/ARB
- Annual: BP, U&E, eGFR, ACR, cardiovascular risk assessment

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy
- Stop ACEi/ARB, switch to Labetalol or Nifedipine
- Target <135/85 mmHg

### Chronic Kidney Disease
- First-line: ACEi or ARB (renoprotective)
- Monitor K+ and creatinine closely

### Heart Failure
- ACEi/ARB, Beta-blocker, and MRA indicated
- Avoid Verapamil/Diltiazem

## Safety Netting
Advise patient to seek help if:
- Severe headache with visual disturbance
- Chest pain or breathlessness
- New weakness or numbness
- Persistent dizziness or fainting
- Significant side effects from medication
