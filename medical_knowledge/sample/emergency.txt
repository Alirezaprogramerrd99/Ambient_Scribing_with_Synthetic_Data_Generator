# Emergency Medicine Protocols

## Sepsis (The Sepsis Six)
### Recognition (qSOFA)
- Respiratory rate ≥22/min.
- Altered mental status (GCS <15).
- Systolic blood pressure ≤100 mmHg.
### Management (The Sepsis Six - Deliver within 1 hour)
**Three In:**
1. **Oxygen**: Maintain saturations >94% (88-92% in COPD).
2. **IV Antibiotics**: Broad spectrum as per local guidelines.
3. **IV Fluids**: Fluid challenge (e.g., 500ml Hartmann's/Saline stat).
**Three Out:**
4. **Blood Cultures**: Take before antibiotics.
5. **Lactate**: Venous or arterial gas.
6. **Urine Output**: Monitor hourly (catheterize if necessary).

## Anaphylaxis
### Symptoms
- Sudden onset.
- Airway: Swelling of tongue/throat, stridor.
- Breathing: Wheeze, dyspnoea.
- Circulation: Hypotension, tachycardia.
- Skin: Urticaria (hives), angioedema.
### Management
1. **Adrenaline (IM)**: 1:1000 concentration.
   - Adult: 500 micrograms (0.5ml).
   - Child (6-12): 300 micrograms (0.3ml).
   - Child (<6): 150 micrograms (0.15ml).
2. High-flow oxygen.
3. IV Fluids.
4. Chlorphenamine (Antihistamine) and Hydrocortisone (Steroid) - *Secondary to Adrenaline*.

## Trauma: Primary Survey (<c>ABCDE)
- **<c> Catastrophic Haemorrhage**: Control massive external bleeding (tourniquet/pressure) first.
- **A - Airway**: Check patency with C-spine control.
- **B - Breathing**: Rate, effort, oxygenation. Rule out tension pneumothorax.
- **C - Circulation**: Pulse, BP, capillary refill, external bleeding.
- **D - Disability**: GCS, pupil response, blood glucose.
- **E - Exposure**: Full examination, prevent hypothermia.
