# Dermatology Assessment Guidelines

## Overview
Skin conditions are extremely common in primary care. This guide covers the most 
frequent presentations and their management.

## Red Flags - Urgent Referral Required
- Suspected melanoma (ABCDE criteria)
- Rapidly changing mole
- Non-healing ulcer
- Severe blistering conditions
- Suspected necrotising fasciitis
- Erythroderma (>90% skin involvement)

## Melanoma Detection (ABCDE)
- Asymmetry: one half unlike the other
- Border: irregular, poorly defined
- Colour: varies from one area to another
- Diameter: >6mm (though can be smaller)
- Evolution: changing size, shape, or colour

## Common Presentations

### Eczema/Dermatitis
- Itchy, red, dry skin
- Flexural distribution common
- May have vesicles, weeping, crusting

Management:
1. Emollients: Apply liberally, multiple times daily
2. Topical steroids:
   - Mild (face/flexures): Hydrocortisone 1%
   - Moderate (body): Betnovate RD, Eumovate
   - Potent (palms/soles/scalp): Betnovate, Dermovate
3. Treat infection if present (Flucloxacillin)
4. Antihistamines for itch (Cetirizine, Chlorphenamine)

### Psoriasis
- Well-demarcated erythematous plaques
- Silver scale
- Commonly affects elbows, knees, scalp, lower back

Management:
1. Emollients
2. Topical vitamin D analogues (Calcipotriol)
3. Topical steroids
4. Combination products (Dovobet)
5. Refer for severe/resistant cases

### Acne
- Comedones, papules, pustules, nodules
- Affects face, chest, back

Management:
- Mild: Benzoyl peroxide 5%, topical retinoids
- Moderate: Add topical antibiotic (Clindamycin)
- Severe: Oral antibiotics (Lymecycline 408mg OD for 3 months)
- Consider COC in females
- Refer for isotretinoin if severe/scarring

### Fungal Infections

#### Tinea (Ringworm)
- Annular lesion with raised scaly edge
- Central clearing
- Treatment: Terbinafine cream BD for 2 weeks

#### Tinea Pedis (Athlete's foot)
- Itchy, macerated skin between toes
- Treatment: Clotrimazole or Terbinafine cream for 2-4 weeks

#### Tinea Unguium (Nail fungus)
- Thickened, discoloured, crumbly nails
- Treatment: Terbinafine 250mg OD for 6-12 weeks

### Urticaria (Hives)
- Itchy wheals that resolve within 24 hours
- Individual lesions last <24 hours
- Acute: usually resolves within 6 weeks
- Chronic: >6 weeks duration

Management:
- Identify and avoid triggers
- Non-sedating antihistamine (Cetirizine 10mg, Fexofenadine 180mg)
- Can increase dose up to 4x licensed dose
- Short course oral steroids for severe flares

### Cellulitis
- Spreading erythema, warmth, swelling
- Often lower leg
- May have systemic symptoms

Management:
- Flucloxacillin 500mg QDS for 7 days
- If penicillin allergic: Clarithromycin 500mg BD
- Mark extent of erythema
- Elevate affected limb
- Admit if: systemically unwell, facial cellulitis, not responding

## Topical Steroid Guide

### Potency Ladder
1. Mild: Hydrocortisone 0.5-1%
2. Moderate: Clobetasone (Eumovate)
3. Potent: Betamethasone (Betnovate)
4. Very potent: Clobetasol (Dermovate)

### Application Rules
- Use lowest effective potency
- Fingertip unit (FTU) dosing
- Once daily usually sufficient
- Step down after control achieved
- Avoid long-term potent steroids on face

## Safety Netting
Advise patient to return if:
- Symptoms not improving after 2 weeks
- Signs of infection (increasing redness, pus, fever)
- New or changing skin lesions
- Severe or spreading rash
- Blistering or skin peeling
