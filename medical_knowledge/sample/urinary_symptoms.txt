# Urinary Symptoms Assessment Guidelines

## Overview
Urinary symptoms are common in both men and women, ranging from infections to 
malignancy. Systematic assessment is essential.

## Red Flags - Urgent Referral Required
- Visible haematuria (2WW cancer pathway)
- Microscopic haematuria with age >60
- Recurrent UTI with haematuria
- Palpable bladder
- Acute urinary retention
- Renal mass on imaging
- Unexplained lower urinary tract symptoms in men >45

## Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

### Presentation
- Dysuria (painful urination)
- Frequency and urgency
- Suprapubic pain
- Haematuria
- Offensive/cloudy urine

### Diagnosis
- Urine dipstick: Nitrites and/or leucocytes
- MSU if: recurrent, pregnant, male, atypical symptoms, treatment failure

### Uncomplicated UTI in Women
- Nitrofurantoin 100mg BD for 3 days (avoid if eGFR <45)
- Or Trimethoprim 200mg BD for 3 days
- Or Pivmecillinam 400mg stat then 200mg TDS for 3 days

### UTI in Men
- Always send MSU
- Trimethoprim 200mg BD for 7 days
- Or Nitrofurantoin 100mg BD for 7 days
- Consider prostatitis if recurrent

### UTI in Pregnancy
- Send MSU
- Nitrofurantoin 100mg BD for 7 days (avoid at term)
- Or Cefalexin 500mg BD for 7 days
- Treat asymptomatic bacteriuria

### Recurrent UTI (≥3/year)
- Urine culture each episode
- Consider prophylaxis: Nitrofurantoin 50-100mg nocte
- Post-coital prophylaxis if related
- Refer if persistent

## Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms (LUTS) in Men

### Storage Symptoms
- Frequency
- Urgency
- Nocturia
- Incontinence

### Voiding Symptoms
- Hesitancy
- Poor stream
- Intermittency
- Terminal dribbling
- Incomplete emptying

### Assessment
- IPSS score (International Prostate Symptom Score)
- Digital rectal examination
- PSA (after counselling)
- Flow rate and post-void residual if available
- U&E (check for renal impairment)

### Management of BPH
1. Watchful waiting if mild symptoms
2. Alpha-blocker (Tamsulosin 400mcg OD) - rapid onset
3. 5-alpha reductase inhibitor (Finasteride 5mg OD) - if prostate >30g
4. Combination therapy for larger prostates
5. Refer for surgery if medical treatment fails

## Overactive Bladder (OAB)

### Symptoms
- Urgency (main symptom)
- Frequency (>8 voids/day)
- Nocturia (>1/night)
- With or without urgency incontinence

### Management
1. Bladder training (first-line)
2. Reduce caffeine and alcohol
3. Anticholinergic: Oxybutynin 2.5-5mg TDS, Solifenacin 5mg OD
4. Beta-3 agonist: Mirabegron 50mg OD (if anticholinergics contraindicated)

## Haematuria

### Visible (Macroscopic)
- Always investigate
- Refer via 2WW pathway if:
  - Age ≥45 with unexplained visible haematuria
  - Age ≥60 with unexplained non-visible haematuria + dysuria/raised WCC
- Urgent CT urogram and cystoscopy

### Non-Visible (Microscopic)
- Confirm on 2 of 3 samples
- Check BP and renal function
- Refer if: age ≥60, proteinuria, reduced eGFR, hypertension

## Safety Netting
Advise patient to return if:
- Symptoms not improving within 48 hours of antibiotics
- Fever, loin pain, vomiting (pyelonephritis)
- Blood in urine
- Unable to pass urine
- Significant increase in symptoms
