# Common Pediatric Illnesses

## Otitis Media (Ear Infection)
### Presentation
- Earache, tugging at ear, fever, irritability, poor feeding.
- Red, bulging tympanic membrane on otoscopy.
### Management
- **Analgesia**: Paracetamol and Ibuprofen are the mainstay.
- **Antibiotics**: Usually not required (viral). Prescribe Amoxicillin ONLY if:
  - Systemically unwell.
  - Perforation (discharge).
  - Bilateral infection in child <2 years.
  - Symptoms persist >3 days.

## Tonsillitis
### Presentation
- Sore throat, fever, difficulty swallowing (dysphagia).
- Enlarged, inflamed tonsils with exudate (pus).
### Centor Criteria (for Antibiotics)
1. Fever >38°C.
2. Tonsillar exudate.
3. No cough.
4. Tender anterior cervical lymphadenopathy.
- **Score 3-4**: Consider Phenoxymethylpenicillin (Penicillin V) for 10 days.

## Croup (Laryngotracheobronchitis)
### Presentation
- Barking "seal-like" cough.
- Stridor (high-pitched inspiratory sound).
- Hoarse voice.
- Worse at night.
### Management
- Single dose of Oral Dexamethasone (0.15mg/kg).
- Keep child calm (agitation worsens stridor).
- Red Flag: Drooling + high fever = Suspect Epiglottitis (Emergency).

## Gastroenteritis (Vomiting & Diarrhoea)
### Assessment
- Check for dehydration: Sunken fontanelle, dry mucous membranes, reduced wet nappies, prolonged capillary refill (>2s).
### Management
- **Fluid Challenge**: Oral Rehydration Solution (small amounts, frequently).
- 5ml every 5 mins if vomiting.
- Maintain breastfeeding.
- **Avoid**: Antidiarrheals (e.g., Loperamide) in children under 12.
