# Gastroenterology Assessment Guidelines

## Dyspepsia and GORD (Reflux)
### Overview
Common condition characterized by upper abdominal discomfort, heartburn, or acid regurgitation.

### Management Steps
- **Conservative**: Weight loss, smoking cessation, smaller meals, avoid triggers (spicy food, caffeine, alcohol).
- **Medical**: 
  - Full dose PPI (e.g., Omeprazole 20mg OD) for 4-8 weeks.
  - Test and treat for H. Pylori if persistent.

### Red Flags (2-Week Wait Referral)
- Dysphagia (difficulty swallowing)
- Unexplained weight loss
- Persistent vomiting
- Epigastric mass
- Age >55 with new onset dyspepsia

## Gallstone Disease (Biliary Colic)
### Presentation
- Colicky pain in Right Upper Quadrant (RUQ) or Epigastrium.
- **Triggers**: Often occurs after eating **fatty foods**.
- Associated with nausea and vomiting.
- Risk Factors: "5 F's" (Fat, Female, Forty, Fair, Fertile).

### Management
- Analgesia (NSAIDs preferred if no contraindications).
- Lifestyle: Low-fat diet.
- Investigation: Abdominal Ultrasound (Gold Standard).
- Referral: Surgical referral for cholecystectomy if symptomatic.

## Appendicitis
### Presentation
- Periumbilical pain migrating to Right Iliac Fossa (RIF).
- Anorexia (loss of appetite), nausea, vomiting.
- Fever and tachycardia.
### Physical Exam
- Tenderness at McBurney's point.
- Rosving's sign (palpation of LLQ causes pain in RLQ).
- Guarding or rebound tenderness (peritonism).

## Acute Diarrhoea and Vomiting (Gastroenteritis)
### Assessment
- Determine hydration status (mucous membranes, urine output, capillary refill).
- Assess for blood in stool (dysentery).
### Management
- **Fluid Rehydration**: Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS) preferred.
- **Diet**: Eat light meals as tolerated. Avoid dairy if lactose intolerance suspected temporarily.
- **Medication**: Loperamide (Imodium) 4mg initially, then 2mg after each loose stool (Avoid in bloody diarrhoea).
- **Safety Netting**: Return if unable to keep fluids down, blood in stool, or severe abdominal pain.
