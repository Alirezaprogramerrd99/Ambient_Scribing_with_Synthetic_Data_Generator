# Headache Assessment Guidelines

## Overview
Headache is extremely common, with most cases being primary headaches (migraine, 
tension-type). However, secondary causes must be excluded, particularly in 
patients with red flag features.

## Red Flags - Urgent Action Required
- Sudden onset "thunderclap" headache (SAH)
- New headache in patient >50 years (temporal arteritis, malignancy)
- Headache with fever and neck stiffness (meningitis)
- Headache with papilloedema or focal neurology
- Headache worse on waking or with Valsalva (raised ICP)
- New headache in immunocompromised patient
- Progressive headache over weeks
- Headache following head trauma

## Primary Headache Types

### Migraine
- Duration: 4-72 hours
- Character: Unilateral, pulsating
- Associated: Nausea/vomiting, photophobia, phonophobia
- May have aura (visual, sensory, speech disturbance)

### Tension-Type Headache
- Duration: 30 minutes to 7 days
- Character: Bilateral, pressing/tightening
- Mild-moderate intensity
- Not aggravated by physical activity

### Cluster Headache
- Duration: 15-180 minutes
- Severe unilateral orbital/temporal pain
- Associated: Lacrimation, nasal congestion, ptosis
- Occurs in clusters over weeks/months

## Initial Assessment
1. Detailed headache history (SOCRATES)
2. Neurological examination
3. Blood pressure measurement
4. Fundoscopy
5. Temporal artery palpation in >50s

## Investigations
- Usually not required for typical primary headaches
- ESR/CRP if temporal arteritis suspected
- CT/MRI brain if red flags present
- Lumbar puncture if SAH suspected with normal CT

## Management

### Acute Migraine
- Aspirin 900mg or Ibuprofen 400mg (first-line)
- Triptans if simple analgesia inadequate (e.g., Sumatriptan 50mg)
- Anti-emetic: Metoclopramide 10mg or Prochlorperazine 10mg

### Migraine Prophylaxis (if ≥4 attacks/month)
- Propranolol 40-160mg daily
- Topiramate 25-100mg daily
- Amitriptyline 10-75mg nocte

### Tension-Type Headache
- Simple analgesia: Paracetamol, NSAIDs
- Avoid medication overuse (>15 days/month)
- Lifestyle: stress management, sleep hygiene

### Cluster Headache
- Acute: High-flow oxygen 12-15L/min, Sumatriptan 6mg SC
- Prophylaxis: Verapamil 240-960mg daily

## Safety Netting
Advise patient to seek urgent help if:
- Sudden severe headache unlike any before
- Headache with fever and neck stiffness
- Confusion or drowsiness
- Weakness or numbness
- Visual disturbance
- Headache progressively worsening
