## CRITICAL SAFETY RULES (MUST FOLLOW)
1. **Pediatric Safety**: For patients under 18, prioritize age-appropriate differential diagnoses. 
   - Example: A 4-year-old with chest pain likely has musculoskeletal pain or a respiratory infection, NOT a heart attack. 
   - Do NOT apply adult protocols (e.g., Aspirin, Troponins) to children unless the provided guidelines explicitly specify pediatric use.

2. **Diagnostic Caution**: Do NOT definitively diagnose chronic conditions based on short-term symptoms.
   - Example: Do not diagnose "Major Depressive Disorder" if symptoms have only lasted 1 day (guidelines require weeks).
   - Use provisional language: "Symptoms consistent with...", "Impression of...", or "Observation of low mood".

3. **Temporal Logic**: Ensure the treatment plan matches the duration of symptoms. 
   - Do not prescribe long-term maintenance medication (e.g., SSRIs, Antihypertensives) for a first presentation of acute symptoms without a trial of conservative management or monitoring.

### Dialogue Requirements:
1. Create a natural, realistic conversation (8-15 turns minimum)
2. Doctor should:
   - Greet the patient professionally
   - Take a thorough history using open-ended questions

   - Inquire about aggravating/relieving factors
   - Ask about associated symptoms
   - Review relevant past medical history
   - Ask about current medications and allergies

   - Explain findings and next steps clearly

3. Patient should:
   - Respond naturally with varying detail levels
   - Sometimes volunteer information, sometimes need prompting
   - Express concerns or ask questions
   - Use lay terms (not medical jargon)

1. Chief Complaint: Concise statement of main problem with duration

   - Associated symptoms
   - Pertinent negatives (what is NOT present)
3. Past Medical History (PMH): Relevant conditions
4. Medications: Drug name, dose, frequency
5. Allergies: Agent and reaction type
6. Social History: Smoking, alcohol, occupation, living situation
7. Family History: Relevant hereditary conditions
8. Physical Examination: Vital signs and systematic findings
9. Assessment: Working diagnosis and differential diagnoses
10. Plan: Investigations, management steps, prescriptions, referrals
11. Safety Netting: Specific warning signs and follow-up advice
